PART I — RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This Residential Lease Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2024 ("Effective Date"), by and between Rajesh Kumar Sharma, residing at 14-B, Vasant Vihar, New Delhi 110057 ("Landlord"), and Priya Mehta, residing at 22, Lajpat Nagar Colony, New Delhi 110024 ("Tenant").

1. TERM OF LEASE
The lease shall commence on February 1, 2024, and shall continue for a fixed term of twelve (12) months, expiring on January 31, 2025. The Tenant shall vacate the premises no later than 11:59 PM on the expiration date unless the parties execute a written renewal agreement at least 30 days prior to the expiration date.

2. RENT AND PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS
The Tenant shall pay monthly rent of INR 45,000 (Forty-Five Thousand Rupees Only) on or before the 5th day of each calendar month. In the event of late payment, the Tenant shall be liable for a penalty of INR 500 per day for each day beyond the 5th. The Tenant must provide post-dated cheques for all 12 months at the time of signing this Agreement. The Landlord shall not be obligated to send reminders or invoices for rent due.

3. SECURITY DEPOSIT
The Tenant agrees to pay a refundable security deposit of INR 1,35,000 (One Lakh Thirty-Five Thousand Rupees Only) equivalent to three months' rent prior to occupancy. The Landlord may deduct from the deposit any unpaid rent, repair costs exceeding normal wear and tear, utility dues, or cleaning charges. The remaining deposit shall be refunded within 30 days of the Tenant vacating the premises.

4. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
The Tenant shall be solely responsible for minor repairs not exceeding INR 2,000 per incident. The Landlord shall be responsible for structural repairs, plumbing failures, and electrical faults notified in writing within 48 hours of occurrence. The Tenant shall not make any structural modifications, paint walls, or install fixtures without prior written consent of the Landlord. Any unauthorised alterations shall entitle the Landlord to forfeit the security deposit in full.

5. TERMINATION AND EVICTION
Either party may terminate this Agreement by providing 60 days' written notice to the other party. In cases of non-payment of rent for more than 15 days, the Landlord may issue a legal eviction notice under the Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958. Upon receipt of such notice, the Tenant must vacate the premises within 30 days or face legal proceedings before the Rent Controller. The Landlord reserves the right to seek damages for unlawful holdover at a rate of 1.5x the monthly rent per month of overstay.

6. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
The Tenant shall not sublet, assign, or transfer occupancy of the premises to any third party without written consent. The Tenant shall not use the premises for any commercial, illegal, or immoral purpose. Keeping pets is strictly prohibited. Violation of this clause shall constitute grounds for immediate termination of the lease without notice.

Should Vikram Anand Verma be unable or unwilling to act, I appoint my daughter, Sunita Anand Kapoor, as the alternate Executor. I bequeath my savings in HDFC Bank Account No. XXXX-4521 equally to my two children, Vikram Anand Verma and Sunita Anand Kapoor. My personal jewellery collection shall be transferred entirely to Sunita Anand Kapoor. IN THE COURT OF THE CIVIL JUDGE (SENIOR DIVISION), NEW DELHI
Civil Suit No. 412/2024

Plaintiff: M/s Bharat Tech Solutions Pvt. Ltd., 7th Floor, Tower C, DLF Cyber City, Gurugram 122002
Defendant: Surendra Nath Pillai, 33, Model Town, Delhi 110009

12. NATURE OF CLAIM
The Plaintiff instituted this suit on March 15, 2024, for recovery of INR 18,75,000 (Eighteen Lakh Seventy-Five Thousand Rupees) being the outstanding consideration due under a Software Development Agreement dated June 10, 2023 ("SDA"). The Defendant agreed to pay the said amount in three equal instalments of INR 6,25,000 each on September 30, 2023, December 31, 2023, and March 31, 2024. The Defendant has wilfully defaulted on all three instalments.

13. STATEMENT OF FACTS
The Plaintiff delivered all software modules as per Schedule A of the SDA on or before the agreed milestones. The Defendant accepted delivery without raising any objection within the prescribed 14-day inspection window, thereby waiving the right to reject the deliverables. Despite repeated written demands dated October 15, 2023, January 8, 2024, and February 20, 2024, the Defendant has failed, neglected, and refused to make any payment.

14. PRAYER FOR RELIEF
The Plaintiff respectfully prays that this Hon'ble Court decree: (a) recovery of INR 18,75,000 as principal outstanding; (b) interest at 18% per annum from the date of default until realisation; (c) costs of this suit; and (d) such other relief as this Court deems fit and proper. The Plaintiff further prays for an interim injunction restraining the Defendant from alienating, transferring, or encumbering his movable and immovable assets pending disposal of the suit.

15. LIMITATION AND JURISDICTION
This suit is filed within the period of limitation prescribed under Article 37 of the Limitation Act, 1963. This Court has territorial and pecuniary jurisdiction to try this suit as the cause of action arose in Delhi and the suit value exceeds INR 10,00,000.

16. INDEMNIFICATION
Each party ("Indemnifying Party") shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the other party, its officers, directors, employees, agents, and successors ("Indemnified Party") from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, penalties, fines, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (i) any breach of this Agreement by the Indemnifying Party; (ii) any negligence or wilful misconduct; or (iii) any infringement of third-party intellectual property rights.

17. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
In no event shall either party be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, punitive, or exemplary damages, including but not limited to loss of profits, loss of goodwill, data loss, or business interruption, even if advised of the possibility of such damages. The maximum aggregate liability of either party under this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

18. FORCE MAJEURE
Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, floods, earthquakes, epidemics, pandemics, war, civil unrest, government orders, or power failures. The affected party must notify the other in writing within 7 days of the occurrence of a force majeure event. If the force majeure condition persists for more than 90 days, either party may terminate this Agreement without penalty.

19. CONFIDENTIALITY
All information exchanged between the parties in connection with this Agreement, whether oral or written, shall be treated as strictly confidential. Neither party shall disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent. This obligation shall survive the termination or expiry of this Agreement for a period of 5 years. Breach of confidentiality shall entitle the non-breaching party to seek injunctive relief without the requirement of posting a bond.

20. DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND ARBITRATION
Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including questions of breach, termination, or invalidity, shall first be attempted to be resolved through good-faith negotiation within 30 days of written notice. Failing amicable resolution, all disputes shall be finally settled by arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, by a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual consent. The seat and venue of arbitration shall be New Delhi. The language of arbitration shall be English. The arbitral award shall be final and binding on both parties.

21. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts in New Delhi for any matters not subject to arbitration.
